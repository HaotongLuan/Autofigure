📊 Executive Overview

The automated generation of scientific figures has emerged as a critical frontier in AI-assisted research. 2026 marks a watershed moment: multiple high-impact papers at ICLR, CVPR, and ACL demonstrate that multi-agent LLM frameworks can produce publication-quality illustrations, while the broader AI Scientist revolution demands automated figure generation as an essential component of fully autonomous research pipelines.

🍌 PaperBanana

Automating Academic Illustration for AI Scientists — A multi-agent framework that generates publication-quality methodology diagrams and statistical plots from paper text.

PaperBanana: Automating Academic Illustration for AI Scientists

Dawei Zhu, Rui Meng, Yale Song, Xiyu Wei, Sujian Li, Tomas Pfister, Jinsung Yoon
Peking University & Google Cloud AI Research
arXiv 2601.23265
Multi-Agent Raster Output VLM-as-Judge Self-Critique Retrieval-Augmented

Architecture: 5-Agent Pipeline

Retriever→ Planner→ Stylist→ Visualizer⟲ Critic→ Figure
AgentRole
RetrieverFinds semantically relevant reference diagrams from curated corpora to ground the generation
PlannerTranslates method descriptions + captions into structured content plans with spatial layout
StylistApplies NeurIPS-style aesthetic conventions: color palettes, typography, iconography
VisualizerCalls image generation models (Gemini-3-Pro, Nano-Banana-Pro) to render the final PNG
CriticIterative self-critique loop: scores output on 4 dimensions, feeds revision instructions back to Visualizer

Key Results (PaperBananaBench: 292 NeurIPS 2025 test cases)

MetricImprovementDescription
Faithfulness+23.7%Factual alignment with source content
Conciseness+18.4%Absence of redundant or irrelevant elements
Readability+31.2%Clarity of visual hierarchy and typography
Aesthetics+27.1%Overall visual appeal per academic standards

Limitations: Output is raster (4K PNG), not vector — limiting downstream editing. Fine-grained faithfulness still imperfect (arrow offsets, boundary blur). Human verification still required for publication use.

🎨 AutoFigure

Generating and Refining Publication-Ready Scientific Illustrations — An agentic framework with iterative Review-Refine loop, accepted at ICLR 2026. Produces editable SVG/mxGraph output.

AutoFigure: Generating and Refining Publication-Ready Scientific Illustrations

Minjun Zhu, Zhen Lin, Yixuan Weng, Panzhong Lu, Qiujie Xie, Yifan Wei, Sifan Liu, Qiyao Sun, Yue Zhang
Westlake University (西湖大学)
ICLR 2026
Dual-Agent SVG Output Review-Refine Loop FigureBench

Architecture: Reasoned Rendering Paradigm

Long Text Input→ Generator Agent⟲ Critic Agent→ SVG / mxGraph

Generator creates initial SVG/mxGraph XML from text → Critic scores (0–10) + provides feedback → Iterate until publication-ready. Decouples semantic layout planning from aesthetic rendering.

🎬 LottieGPT

Tokenizing Vector Animation for Autoregressive Generation — The first framework to tokenize and autoregressively generate editable vector animations, accepted at CVPR 2026. Extends the AutoSciFigure pipeline into the temporal domain.

LottieGPT: Tokenizing Vector Animation for Autoregressive Generation

Junhao Chen, Kejun Gao, Yuehan Cui, Mingze Sun, Mingjin Chen, Shaohui Wang, Xiaoxiao Long, Fei Ma, Qi Tian, Ruqi Huang, Hao Zhao
Tsinghua University, BAAI, Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Nanjing University, Guangdong Lab of AI & Digital Economy (SZ)
CVPR 2026
Autoregressive Vector Animation Lottie Format Tokenization Qwen-VL Multi-Modal

Architecture: Three-Stage Pipeline

Lottie Tokenizer→ LottieAnimation-660K→ LottieGPT (Qwen-VL)→ Editable Animation

Key Components

ComponentDescription
Lottie Tokenizer Encodes layered geometric primitives (shapes, paths), transforms, and keyframe motion from Lottie JSON into compact semantic token sequences. ~34% sequence length reduction via JSON simplification.
LottieAnimation-660K 660K real-world Lottie animations + 15M static Lottie images. Largest vector animation dataset. ~608 hours of animation; avg 3.26 sec / 104.7 frames. Captions via BLIP-2 + Qwen2.5-VL 32B.
LottieGPT Model Fine-tuned Qwen-VL that autoregressively generates Lottie token sequences from text, images, or keyframes. Outputs fully editable, resolution-independent vector animations.

Why Lottie Format?

PropertyAdvantage
JSON-basedNaturally tokenizable; structured command-parameter format
Resolution-independentVector graphics scale to any size without quality loss
EditableShapes, colors, motion curves can be modified post-generation
Tiny file sizeTypically a few KB vs MB for raster video
Web-nativeRenders natively in all browsers, iOS, Android

Relationship to SciFigure Generation

While LottieGPT targets general vector animation, its tokenization strategy is directly relevant to scientific illustration: animated diagrams (network flows, algorithm visualizations, process animations) are increasingly common in digital publications. The autoregressive tokenization paradigm can be adapted to generate animated scientific figures with temporal dynamics.

Related competitor: OmniLottie (arXiv:2603.02138, CVPR 2026) — parameterized Lottie tokens with MMLottie-2M dataset (2M animations), 97% generation success rate.

🔗 Related Works & Research Ecosystem

The three anchor papers sit within a rapidly expanding ecosystem. Below we catalog related 2026 works organized by research thrust.

1. Direct Extensions & Successors

AutoFigure-Edit

arXiv 2603.06674
Zhen Lin, Qiujie Xie, Minjun Zhu et al. (13 authors)

Extends AutoFigure with SAM3 segmentation, RMBG-2.0 background removal, and a placeholder-to-SVG assembly pipeline for fully editable scientific illustrations. Supports reference-guided style transfer and multi-modal conditioning. GitHub

FigAgent

arXiv 2603.29590
Zhuoling Li et al.

Multi-agent framework specifically for AI paper method illustrations. Novel Explore-and-Select drawing strategy mimics human trial-and-error sketching. Encapsulates reusable drawing middlewares that evolve dynamically. Project Page

OmniLottie

CVPR 2026
Yiying Yang et al.

Competing vector animation framework. Parameterized Lottie token design (command + parameter sequences). MMLottie-2M dataset (2M professional animations). 97%+ generation success rate with Qwen2.5-VL fine-tuning.

ScholarPeer

Google Research, Apr 2026
Jinsung Yoon, Tomas Pfister (Google Cloud)

Sister system to PaperBanana. Multi-agent peer review framework with Sub-domain Historian + Baseline Scout + Q&A Engine. Drastically reduces gap between AI and human review diversity. Part of Google's vision for AI-assisted academic lifecycle.

2. Benchmarks & Evaluation

SciFlow-Bench

ACL 2026 Main
Tong Zhang, Honglin Lin, Zhou Liu, Chong Chen, Wentao Zhang

Structure-aware evaluation via inverse parsing: converts generated diagrams back into structured graphs, then compares to ground truth. Uses a hierarchical multi-agent system for planning, perception, and structural reasoning. Addresses the "looks right but wrong" problem.

DiagramBank

arXiv 2604.20857
Tingwen Zhang et al.

89,422 schematic diagrams from top-tier publications with paper metadata. CLIP-based filtering to distinguish schematics from standard plots. RAG codebase for exemplar-conditioned figure synthesis. GitHub

SciDraw-6K

arXiv 2604.17206
(Multi-institutional)

6,291 scientific illustrations generated by Gemini models across 11 languages and 8 domains (biomedical, chemistry, materials, electronics, environmental, AI systems, physics). Multilingual benchmark for illustration quality.

3. SVG & Vector Graphics Generation

IntroSVG

arXiv 2603.09312
Feiyu Wang et al.

Unified VLM that is both generator and critic. SFT for SVG drafting + feedback, DPO with teacher VLM for refinement. Iterative "generate-review-refine" at inference. SOTA on complex SVG structure and semantic alignment.

VecGlypher

CVPR 2026
Xiaoke Huang, Bhavul Gauri et al. (Meta AI)

Single multimodal LLM that autoregressively emits SVG path tokens from text or image exemplars. Two-stage training: 39K noisy fonts → 2.5K expert-annotated Google Fonts. Avoids raster intermediates entirely.

HiVG (Hierarchical SVG Tokenization)

arXiv 2604.05072
Ximing Xing, Ziteng Xue et al.

Hierarchical tokenizer decomposing SVG into atomic tokens (structure, commands, coordinates, attributes), then compressing into segment tokens for 63.8% sequence reduction. Hierarchical Mean-Noise initialization to combat coordinate hallucination.

4. System Architecture & Diagram Generation

Paper2SysArch

CVPR 2026 Findings
Ziyi Guo, Zhou Liu, Wentao Zhang

Generates system architecture diagrams from scientific papers with explicit structural constraints. Part of the Paper2Any ecosystem.

SciDoc2Diagrammer-MAF

EMNLP 2024 Findings
Ishani Mondal et al.

Foundational work. Multi-Aspect Feedback refinement strategy for generating scientific diagrams from documents. Introduced SciDoc2DiagramBench. GitHub

🔮 Key Trends & Future Directions

Synthesis of major patterns observed across the 2026 AutoSciFigure literature.

🤖

Multi-Agent Architectures

All major systems (PaperBanana, AutoFigure, FigAgent) adopt multi-agent designs. Specialized agents (Planner, Stylist, Critic, Visualizer) outperform monolithic end-to-end models. The critic agent is the single most important component for output quality.

📐

Raster → Vector Transition

PaperBanana outputs raster PNG, but AutoFigure-Edit, VecGlypher, HiVG, and LottieGPT all target editable vector formats. The community consensus is clear: editable vectors are the end goal. Raster is a stepping stone.

🔄

Iterative Self-Refinement

Critique-and-Refine loops are universal. PaperBanana's Critic, AutoFigure's Review-Refine, IntroSVG's generator-critic DPO all converge on the same pattern: generation is necessary but insufficient; refinement is what delivers quality.

📊

Standardized Benchmarks

The field is maturing from qualitative demos to quantitative benchmarks: PaperBananaBench (292), FigureBench (3,300), SciFlow-Bench (structure-aware), DiagramBank (89K+). VLM-as-Judge and inverse parsing are emerging as scalable evaluation paradigms.

🧩

Tokenization as the Bridge

LottieGPT, OmniLottie, HiVG, and VecGlypher all converge on the same insight: tokenizing structured visual formats (SVG, Lottie JSON) into autoregressive token sequences enables LLMs to "speak vector graphics." This is the most promising technical direction for editable, high-quality output.

🌐

Integration into AI Scientist Pipelines

AutoFigure is explicitly part of DeepScientist. PaperBanana pairs with ScholarPeer. The AI Scientist (Nature 2026) includes figure generation as a core module. AutoSciFigure is no longer a standalone task — it's becoming a component of end-to-end autonomous research.

🎯

Structural Correctness over Aesthetics

SciFlow-Bench's inverse parsing approach and PaperBanana's faithfulness metric reveal that the hardest problem is not making figures beautiful — it's making them structurally correct. Arrow directions, module boundaries, and component relationships are where current systems still fail.

🏛️

Policy & Governance Emerging

The 2026 survey on AI-generated figures in academic publishing (arXiv:2603.16159) signals that publishers and institutions are beginning to establish policies. Transparency, reproducibility, and author accountability are emerging as key governance requirements.

⚠️ Open Challenges

Fine-Grained Faithfulness

Arrow misalignment, missing connections, hallucinated components — all three anchor papers acknowledge that structural precision at the pixel level remains unsolved. Inverse parsing evaluation (SciFlow-Bench) quantifies this gap.

Domain Generalization

Current systems are evaluated primarily on CS/ML papers. Generalization to chemistry (molecular diagrams), biology (pathway figures), physics (Feynman diagrams), and engineering (circuit schematics) is largely unexplored.

Text Rendering Accuracy

AI-generated figures frequently produce garbled, misspelled, or misplaced text labels. OCR-based erase-and-correct post-processing helps but doesn't fully solve the problem.

Animation Fidelity

LottieGPT and OmniLottie can generate animations, but temporal consistency, physical plausibility, and semantic correctness over time remain open problems. No benchmark yet exists for scientific animation quality.

Cost & Accessibility

AutoFigure costs ~$0.20/figure via cloud API; PaperBanana requires Gemini-3-Pro access. Making these systems truly accessible to researchers worldwide (especially in resource-constrained settings) remains a challenge.

Evaluation at Scale

Human expert evaluation is the gold standard but doesn't scale. VLM-as-Judge is promising but introduces circularity (using the same VLMs to evaluate VLM output). Structure-aware automated metrics are still nascent.
